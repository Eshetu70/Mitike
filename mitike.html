📋 Buying Process

📑 1. Before Applying for a Loan

💰 Budget for more than just a down payment — include inspections, closing costs, appraisals, and emergency maintenance.

🔧 Set aside funds for tools, repairs, and new appliances after move-in.

📉 PMI Tip: 20% down avoids PMI, but 3.5% FHA options exist. PMI can be removed once you reach 20% equity.

📊 Credit Score Matters:

🔴 Poor: 579 or below

🟠 Fair: 580–669

🟡 Good: 670–739

🟢 Very Good: 740–799

💚 Excellent: 800+

✅ Higher scores mean lower interest rates and reduced monthly payments.

🧾 2. Additional Steps

📈 Improve your credit for better terms and stronger loan approval chances.

📝 Documents You’ll Need: Tax returns, W-2s/1099s, pay stubs, government-issued ID, and bank statements.

⛔ Avoid taking on new debt, changing jobs, or making large purchases before closing.

📝 When Ready, Make an Offer: Submit earnest money (typically 1–3%) to show you're serious.

🔍 Schedule Inspections: General, termite, sewer, radon, and others can strengthen negotiations.

🏁 Closing Day Prep: Bring your ID, certified funds, and confirm utilities are transferred before you get the keys!

💼 Finding a Lender

🤝 Trusted Lending Partners

These are lenders I’ve worked with, closed deals with, and trust based on consistent positive results for my clients. However, you’re always free to compare and choose your own.

🧠 Tip: Terms vary by lender and state. I’ll guide you toward professionals you can rely on — and provide contact info for confidence and clarity.

✅ Going beyond pre-qualification with a pre-approval shows sellers you're financially ready, making your offer stronger and more credible.

📑 Required Documents

  • 🪪 Personal Info: Full name, address, birthdate, SSN
  • 📄 Income: Pay stubs, W-2s/1099s, tax returns (2 yrs)
  • 🏦 Bank Records: 2–3 months of statements

⚠️ Important: Avoid unsourced deposits or large cash movements — sources like EKUBE, EDIR, etc. may cause underwriting delays.

🛑 Avoid new credit cards, auto loans, or large purchases during the process — these affect your credit profile and approval chances.

🏡 Available Loan Programs

You are free to choose your lender — whether it's one I refer or one you’ve researched. Getting pre-qualified and then pre-approved improves your position dramatically.

  • 🎖️ VA Loans: For veterans and active military
  • 🏠 FHA Loans: Ideal for lower credit or small down payments
  • 💰 Conventional Loans: For stronger credit and larger down payments
  • 🏦 Portfolio Loans: Offered by private banks outside conventional standards
  • 📍 Local Programs: MHDC (MO), KHRC (KS) — assistance for down payment & closing costs
  • 🧾 MCC: Mortgage Credit Certificate — tax credit for first-time buyers
  • 📊 Down Payment Toward Equity Act: Up to $25K — pending federal legislation
  • 🎁 Gift Funds: From family — must include proper documentation and gift letter

📘 Types Of FHA Loans

Fixed and adjustable-rate FHA loans are available, including refinance and renovation options (203K).

🏗️ Fixed-Rate FHA Loans

  • 30-year fixed
  • 25-year fixed
  • 20-year fixed
  • 15-year fixed
These offer stable rates. Shorter terms = less interest. Longer terms = lower payments.

🔄 Adjustable-Rate FHA Loans

Lower initial rate for a few years, then adjusts. Best if you plan to move or refinance early.

♻️ FHA Refinance Loan

  • FHA fixed refinance
  • FHA ARM refinance
  • FHA cash-out refinance*
Also allows refinancing from conventional into FHA.

🔧 FHA 203K Renovation Loan

Finance both purchase and home improvements:

Standard 203K:
  • Add new rooms or open layout
  • Major plumbing, roof, foundation
  • Energy upgrades
Limited 203K:
  • Minor HVAC or electrical fixes
  • Non-structural remodeling
  • Energy-efficient installations

🚫 What Not To Do & Final Steps

⚠️ Avoid These Common Mistakes

💼 Do not switch jobs during the mortgage process

💳 Avoid opening new credit accounts or maxing out cards

💸 Don’t deposit or withdraw large amounts without documentation

🛋️ Wait to buy furniture or large items until after you close

🔗 Confirm that your rate is locked with your lender

🔍 Inspections & Offers

Once your offer is accepted and earnest money (typically 1%–3%) is submitted, it’s time to schedule key inspections:

🏚️ General Home Inspection

🐜 Termite Inspection

🚽 Sewer or Septic Inspection

🏗️ Foundation Check

☢️ Radon Test

Use the results to negotiate repairs or credits before closing.

🏁 Appraisal, Closing & Final Safety Tips

🔍 Your lender will order an appraisal. If the home appraises lower than expected, we’ll discuss renegotiation or out-of-pocket options.

🚨 Security Tip: Always verify wire instructions by phone — wire fraud is real. Call before sending any funds.

💡 Before closing, be sure to:

🔌 Transfer utilities to your name

🪪 Prepare your government-issued photo ID

💵 Bring certified closing funds (cashier’s check or wire)
