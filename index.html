Buying Process

1. Before Applying for a Loan

Buying a home involves more than just a down payment. It’s important to budget for inspections, closing costs, appraisals, and unexpected home maintenance. Consider putting aside funds for essential tools, appliances, and emergency repairs.

Most of my clients aim to put 20% down to avoid PMI, though 3.5% options exist. PMI can often be removed once you’ve paid off 20%. Online mortgage calculators are a great tool to estimate what extra monthly payments can help you reach that goal faster.

Credit score is also critical:

  • Poor: 579 or below
  • Fair: 580–669
  • Good: 670–739
  • Very Good: 740–799
  • Excellent: 800+

By knowing your score and improving it, you unlock better rates and lower monthly payments.

2. Additional Steps

  • Improving Credit Score: Crucial for qualifying better rates and terms.
  • Loan Documents Required: Tax returns, pay stubs, W2s/1099s, bank statements, ID, and more.
  • What Not To Do: Avoid job changes, large financial moves, new debt, or changing marital status before closing.
  • Finding Your Home & Making an Offer: Your agent will help strategize your offer. Earnest money (typically 1%-3%) is submitted after acceptance and held by the title company.
  • Inspections: Types include General, Termite, Sewer, Foundation, and Radon. Use findings to negotiate repairs or credits.
  • Title & Closing: The title company clears liens and coordinates closing. You’ll bring a cashier’s check and ID. Utilities should be transferred 3–4 days ahead.

Finding a Lender

Trusted Lending Partners

As mentioned in my introduction, you’re not limited to the lenders I refer, but the professionals I recommend are ones I’ve personally worked with, closed deals with, and have consistently received positive feedback from my clients.

Feel free to compare lenders and evaluate your options — terms can vary by lender and by state. I’ll also provide contact info for qualified professionals who can assist you with confidence.

It's important to go beyond pre-qualification — a pre-approval or preliminary approval strengthens your offer by showing sellers you are financially ready and serious.

Required Documents

  • Personal Information: Full name, address, birthdate, and SSN
  • Income Documentation: Pay stubs, W-2 forms, 1099s, 2 years of tax returns
  • Bank Records: 2-3 months of bank statements

Important: Avoid unsourced deposits or large withdrawals during the loan process — sources like EKUBE, EDIR, etc. can cause delays or denials.

Do not apply for new credit cards, auto loans, or make large purchases during this time, as they affect your credit report and overall loan approval.

Available Loan Programs

You are free to choose your lender—whether recommended or personally researched. Getting pre-qualified and then pre-approved significantly improves your chances of acceptance.

  • VA Loans: For veterans and active military personnel
  • FHA Loans: Ideal for lower credit scores or smaller down payments
  • Conventional Loans: Typically require higher credit scores and larger down payments
  • Portfolio Loans: Offered by private banks and lenders outside conventional guidelines
  • Local Programs: MHDC (Missouri), KHRC (Kansas) – down payment and closing cost support
  • MCC (Mortgage Credit Certificate): Annual tax credit for qualified first-time homebuyers
  • Down Payment Toward Equity Act: Up to $25K – pending federal legislation
  • Gift Funds: From family with documentation and gift letter

What Not To Do & Final Steps

Avoid These Common Mistakes

  • Do not change your name during the process
  • Do not quit your job or change employment
  • Do not renew your lease unexpectedly
  • Do not assume you need 20% down – ask about your options
  • Do not assume your rate is locked unless you’ve confirmed it
  • Do not shop with another agent while under contract
  • Do not deposit or withdraw large amounts of cash
  • Do not change your marital status mid-transaction
  • Do not open or max out new credit cards
  • Do not add new debt or miss payments
  • Do not shop for furniture or home goods before closing

Inspections & Offers

Once you’ve found the right home, we’ll structure an offer. Earnest money (1–3% of the offer) is due within 3 days and applied to closing. Next, schedule inspections:

  • General home inspection
  • Termite inspection
  • Sewer/septic inspection
  • Foundation inspection
  • Radon inspection

After inspection, you can negotiate repairs or credits. Be mindful of timelines to keep the transaction on track.

Appraisal, Closing & Safety

Once past inspection, your lender will order an appraisal to confirm the home’s value. If it appraises below the offer price, we’ll negotiate or determine how to cover the difference.

Be vigilant with your information and avoid scams. Don’t respond to unexpected emails about wiring funds—always confirm with your lender, title company, or agent.

Three days before closing, you’ll receive final instructions. Transfer utilities into your name 3–4 days in advance. Bring your photo ID and cashier’s check to the appointment. Then… congratulations — you're a homeowner!
