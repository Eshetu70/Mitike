Loan Programs

Which Loan is Right for You?

Understanding your options is the first step to making a smart financing decision.

Conventional Loan

Most Common

Standard mortgage backed by private lenders. Down payments as low as 3% for qualified buyers. Best for those with solid credit (620+).

3–20% Down 620+ Credit No PMI at 20%

FHA Loan

First-Time Buyers

Government-backed loan with lower credit requirements. Down payments as low as 3.5%. Excellent option for first-time buyers building credit.

3.5% Down 580+ Credit Flexible DTI

VA Loan

Veterans & Military

Exclusively for eligible veterans, active-duty service members, and surviving spouses. No down payment required. No PMI. Competitive rates.

0% Down No PMI VA Eligible

USDA Loan

Rural Buyers

Zero-down loan for eligible rural and suburban areas. Income limits apply. Parts of the KC metro area qualify — we'll help you check.

0% Down Rural Areas Income Limits

Important Advice

What Not to Do After Pre-Approval

These common mistakes can derail your closing — even after your offer is accepted. Protect your loan by avoiding them.

🚗

Don't Finance a New Car

Large new debts change your debt-to-income ratio and can void your pre-approval. Wait until after closing to make any major purchases.

💳

Don't Open New Credit Cards

Applying for new credit triggers hard inquiries and lowers your score. Even a small drop can change your loan terms or disqualify you.

💼

Don't Change Jobs

Lenders want employment stability. Changing employers — even for more pay — creates underwriting complications that can delay or sink your closing.

🏦

Don't Move Large Sums of Money

Unexplained large transfers raise red flags in underwriting. Keep your accounts stable and document any deposits your lender hasn't seen before.

📦

Don't Co-Sign Any Loans

Co-signing adds someone else's debt to your financial profile. Even if they pay on time, it affects your DTI and can jeopardize your mortgage.

📊

Don't Forget to Pay Bills on Time

Lenders often pull a final credit check before closing. One missed payment in this window can change your interest rate or cost you the loan.

Questions? Talk to us before making any financial decisions during your home purchase.
